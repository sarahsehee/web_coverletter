Project 1

Switcher, I/O Inc

Improving Switcher Timer Experience

Overview

Analyzing users behavior that affects conversion rate with data, and designing a sequential visual guide to introduce Switcher timer in a small scale.

Duration

Aug 2016 - Sep 2016

Switcher is a smart light controller targeting a single or a married couple household in South Korea. The target user is aged from 25 to 35 years old living in a studio or one bed apartment. By hacking the lifestyle of the target user, Switcher has become the most affordable and intuitive smart home device in South Korea. It comes with a set of two velcro strips for easy installation and bluetooth pairing with smartphone to control the light.

Why I focused on Timer

Switcher is a rental service offering four paid subscription plans: monthly, annual, biannual and triennial plans. By trying Switcher for one month, users can decide if it is useful, select one of these four subscription plans to keep using or return Switcher to cancel automattic recurring montly subscription plan.

In August 2016, we ran several rounds of quantitive studies to discover what affects the trial-to-paid conversion rate, and finally found out the correlation between the number of utilized timer and the trial-to-premium conversion. 67.5% of a group of premium users were utilizing more than one timer. In a meanwhile, only 35.3% of a group of users who returned Switchers after the free trial set s timer.

Percentage(%) of users who set and use a timer

Based on the result above, we made a hypothesis that if a user installed and utilized one or more timer during the free trial, she becomes a premium user. To validate, we ran another suvery to premium users, and asked if they are using a timer and which subscription plan they chose. And it showed that users who utilize timer selects longer and more expansive subscription plan than users who doesn't use any timer.

Premium Users' Timer and Plan

Even though we always emphasize the benefit of timer and use the word in our marketing copyright and website, still there are a lot of users who don't use a timer even though it can maximize their smart home device. So we individually interviewed them to figure out the reason why they don't use a timer:

1. Many users don't know about timer at all.
2. Users can't find any information about timer in mobile app.

Design Solution . Version 1

Card Modal

Switcher mobile app does not provide sufficient information about timer in the app. Instead of making a full interactive onboarding on the first set-up, card style modal came out as a solution so that it can appear to users who's been already using Switcher. Also, it was the smallest scale of design and required fewer resources and time. Thus, we designed the card guide as our first step to knowing about user behavior with timer.

Higher Level Goal

To guide user to test and understand the concept of Switcher timer ✅
To suggest a timer use case other than morning-lit-up to user ☀️
To increase the converstion rate of free-to-paid ↗
To increase the average price of first payment after a free-trial ↗

Design Process

I used inVsion to create rough prototypes. These rough prototypes were made for fast iteration and test by myself about timing and the flow of the Cards.

Design Development

After running two rounds of text-only prototypes, I got feedback that card didn't look like something that holds valuable information.

So I designed illustrations on three cards to fill out the card to draw users' attention. At a glance, our users get fun and useful guide to learn new feature about Switcher, which helps to improve the smart home experience for them.

Result

Timer Card Guide Funnel Chart

I assumed the conversion rate of timer guide would be 20% before tracking google analytics, but it appeared that there is a high drop-off rate on Card #2 and Card #3. It gave us an insight that presenting more than two modals are conceived as intrusive in user's behavior and there should be less steps to guide users to timer.

Takeaways

Partnering closely with the marketer to discover about users' pain points by approaching different research methods including survey, interview, and Google Analytics
Prototyping and testing small scale of design to real people and getting actionable feedback
Overseeing the holistic In-App user experience
Designing infographic assets and card user interface elements for future use
